# Sprint Retrospective

## What is a Sprint Retrospective?

The Sprint Retrospective is a meeting where the team reflects on their process and identifies improvements for the next sprint. It's a dedicated time for the team to inspect itself and create a plan for enhancements.

## Sprint Retrospective Overview

- **Duration**: 1-1.5 hours for a 2-week sprint
- **Timing**: After Sprint Review, before next Sprint Planning
- **Participants**: Scrum Team only (Development Team, Scrum Master, Product Owner)
- **Format**: Facilitated discussion focused on improvement
- **Outcome**: Action items for process improvement

## The Purpose

The retrospective helps the team answer:

1. **What went well?** - Identify successes to repeat
2. **What didn't go well?** - Recognize issues to address
3. **What can we improve?** - Create actionable changes

## Sprint Retrospective Agenda

### 1. Set the Stage (5 minutes)
- Welcome everyone
- Review retrospective purpose and goals
- Set ground rules (e.g., "Vegas rule" - what's said here stays here)
- Create safe, open environment

### 2. Gather Data (15 minutes)
- Review sprint metrics and data
- Collect team member observations
- Use retrospective activities (see formats below)

### 3. Generate Insights (20 minutes)
- Discuss patterns and themes
- Identify root causes
- Prioritize issues to address

### 4. Decide What to Do (15 minutes)
- Create specific, actionable improvements
- Assign owners to action items
- Set success criteria

### 5. Close the Retrospective (5 minutes)
- Summarize action items
- Appreciate team contributions
- Confirm next steps

## Popular Retrospective Formats

### Start, Stop, Continue
Team members identify:
- **Start**: What should we begin doing?
- **Stop**: What should we stop doing?
- **Continue**: What should we keep doing?

### Mad, Sad, Glad
Team members share:
- **Mad**: What frustrated us?
- **Sad**: What disappointed us?
- **Glad**: What made us happy?

### 4 Ls
Reflect on:
- **Liked**: What did we enjoy?
- **Learned**: What did we discover?
- **Lacked**: What was missing?
- **Longed For**: What did we wish for?

### Sailboat
Visual metaphor:
- **Wind**: What helped us move forward?
- **Anchor**: What held us back?
- **Rocks**: What risks do we face?
- **Island**: What is our goal?

### Timeline
Create a visual timeline of the sprint:
- Mark significant events
- Note emotional reactions
- Identify patterns over time

## Best Practices

1. **Psychological safety**: Create environment where everyone can speak freely
2. **Focus on process**: Discuss how the team works, not individual performance
3. **Be specific**: Use concrete examples, not generalizations
4. **Look forward**: Focus on improvement, not blame
5. **Actionable items**: Create specific tasks with owners
6. **Follow through**: Review previous action items
7. **Vary the format**: Keep retrospectives fresh and engaging
8. **Timebox discussions**: Stay focused and on schedule
9. **Everyone participates**: Hear from all team members
10. **Document outcomes**: Record and share action items

## Common Challenges

### Challenge: Same issues every sprint
**Solution:** Focus on root causes, not symptoms. Try the "5 Whys" technique.

### Challenge: Low participation
**Solution:** Use different formats, do activities in pairs/small groups first, explicitly ask quiet members for input.

### Challenge: Only complaints, no solutions
**Solution:** Redirect to "What can we do about it?" Follow problem statements with action brainstorming.

### Challenge: Finger-pointing and blame
**Solution:** Reinforce ground rules, reframe as system/process issues, intervene if it continues.

### Challenge: No follow-through on action items
**Solution:** Assign clear owners, review previous actions at start of each retro, limit number of action items.

### Challenge: Running over time
**Solution:** Strict timebox adherence, park detailed discussions for later, focus on top 2-3 issues.
